About DEBRIS

In the face of natural or man-made disaster, urban search and rescue teams and other first responders like police, medical units or civil protection race against the clock to locate survivors within the critical 72-hour timeframe (Golden Hours), facing challenges such as instable structures or hazardous environments but also insufficient situational awareness â€“ all resulting in lengthy SaR processes. In order to speed up the detection of survivors trapped in collapsed buildings and to improve working conditions for the first responders.

DEBRIS project develops a new class of robots designed to navigate and explore underground environments with limited access. Unlike traditional rigid robots, DEBRIS robots incorporate soft and flexible materials, enabling them to traverse complex and confined spaces such as soil, underground pipes, or tunnels. This flexibility allows the robots to move through debris and other obstructions that would be impassable for conventional machines.

The primary objective of DEBRIS (Detection and Exploration Bot for Rescue, Investigation, and Survival) is to develop an advanced, user-friendly Search and Rescue (SaR) system designed to rapidly and accurately detect and locate individuals trapped in disaster zones. DEBRIS aims to significantly reduce response times by utilizing a sophisticated integration of sensor data and real-time camera feeds to create a unified, comprehensive operational overview. This system will support the prioritization and execution of critical actions during SaR missions, thereby enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of rescue operations and increasing the likelihood of saving lives.
